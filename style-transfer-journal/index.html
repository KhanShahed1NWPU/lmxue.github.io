Cycle Consistent Network for End-to-end Style Transfer TTS Training

Authors: Liumeng Xue1, Shifeng Pan2, Lei He2, Lei Xie1, Frank K. Soong2
              1Audio, Speech and Language Processing Group (ASLP@NPU), School of Computer Science, Northwestern Polytechnical University, Xi’an, China
              2Microsoft China
Abstract: In this paper, we propose a cycle network based, end-to-end TTS for improving speaking style transfer training. End-to-end, multi-speaker TTS systems of Variational Autoencoder (VAE) and Global Style Token (GST) are chosen as baseline systems to be compared with the proposed approach in their corresponding style transfer performance. In the proposed approach we augment the VAE baseline model with an unpaired path which is made of unpaired style reference input, variational style encoder and transferred output, to learn the style representation intra- and inter- speakers, jointly with the paired path. An interconnected, cycle consistent network structure is introduced to make the training algorithm tractable. The Ablation tests on two separate style encoders and cycle structure show their effectiveness in style transfer performance. Compared with the baseline systems, the proposed approach is shown to be more effective than both GST and VAE based models in intra- and inter-speaker or unseen speaker style transfer, for both parallel and unparallel transfer.
