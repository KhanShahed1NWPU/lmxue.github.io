Cycle Consistent Network for End-to-end Style Transfer TTS Training

Authors: Liumeng Xue1, Shifeng Pan2, Lei He2, Lei Xie1, Frank K. Soong2
              1Audio, Speech and Language Processing Group (ASLP@NPU), School of Computer Science, Northwestern Polytechnical University, Xi’an, China
              2Microsoft China
Abstract: In this paper, we propose an end-to-end, style transfer TTS. On top of an end-to-end multi-speaker TTS model where Variational Autoencoder (2.VAE) is used to learn the latent representation of the speaking style in speech, we firstly augment it with an unpaired path including unpaired style reference input, variational style encoder and transferred output, to learn cross-speaker style representation, in addition to the paired one. Secondly, a cycle consistency network is proposed to make the unpaired path trainable algorithmically. The Ablation tests on two separate style encoders and cycle structure show their effectiveness. The final evaluations show that the proposed approach is more effective than Global Style Token (1.GST) and 2.VAE based models on intra-speaker, inter-speaker and unseen speaker style transfer, for both parallel transfer and unparallel transfer.
